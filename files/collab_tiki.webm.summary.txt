This talk focused on the increasing popularity of markdown in software development, and the adoption of markdown in the Tiki project. It discussed the history of markdown, including the stalled project of wiki Creole, and the current benchmark of markdown, Common Mark. It also discussed the Tiki-specific plugins so users can do things like centered text, and their switch from CKeditor to ToastUE. The speaker also talked about the version of markdown used by different platforms and the need for a Tiki-flavored version of markdown.
