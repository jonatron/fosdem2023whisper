In this talk, the speaker discusses the road to Int message format, which is an upcoming improvement in localization and message formatting. They explain that localization is often an afterthought in software development and that current localization practices are not optimal. The speaker introduces message format 2, which aims to improve localization by providing a common way of localizing content. They explain the format and its benefits, such as supporting complex message structures, variables, and language-specific features. The speaker also mentions that the work is being standardized and developed in open forums. The talk concludes with a discussion of the challenges and possibilities of message format 2, including its support for different languages and its potential for enabling more complex localization workflows.
